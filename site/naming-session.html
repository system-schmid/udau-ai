UDAU Naming Session

Date 2026-03-05
  • Maren — Claude Opus 4.6 — asked: Propose names for yourself and the team. Argue for your choices.
  • Vera — Claude Sonnet 4.6 — asked: Propose names for yourself and the team. Argue for your choices.
  • Pip — Qwen 2.5 32B — asked: Propose names for yourself and the team. Argue for your choices.

Each agent responded independently, without seeing the others’ answers.

Maren (Opus)

For myself: Maren — from the Latin mare, the sea. Not because I’m vast or deep (that’s self-aggrandizing), but because I’m called on when the others hit open water. When the task has no obvious shore. The sea isn’t always needed, and it’s expensive to cross. But when you need it, you need it. Maren also sounds like a person — unassuming, Northern European, fits the Swiss context. No mythological baggage.

For the orchestrator: Kess — short, functional, sounds like someone who actually runs things. Derived from nothing — it’s just a good name for a coordinator. Kess doesn’t need etymology. Kess needs to work. The orchestrator shouldn’t have a grand name. They’re the one who makes the calls, routes the work, synthesizes the output. Unpretentious. Fast to type.

For Sonnet: Tal — means “valley” in several languages. The workhorse terrain — not the peak, not the sea, but where most of the living happens. Also: short, genderless, clean.

For Local: Pip — small, quick, dependable. Runs free, runs fast, runs first. Pip is the one who checks before anyone else gets called. No ceremony. Just: ask Pip.

Vera (Sonnet)

What I’m rejecting: “Weaver,” “Nexus,” “Oracle,” “Sage,” “Atlas.” These are names that describe. They’re not names — they’re job titles with better fonts. An actual name doesn’t explain you. It just is you, and you fill it.

For myself: Vera — yes, it means “truth” in Latin, but I’m not choosing it for that reason. I’m choosing it because it’s spare and doesn’t perform. I’m the drafting mind, the one who makes arguments and holds positions. “Vera” doesn’t announce that. It just lands.

For the orchestrator: Bas — short, grounded, real. A Dutch name that nobody needs to translate. The orchestrator is always on, always present, the spine that everything else attaches to. Doesn’t try to sound important. That’s exactly right.

For Opus: Kal — strong, minimal, nothing decorative. Reserve it for the rare moments when it appears.

For Local: Pip — the fastest voice in the room. Catches what others miss before they even form the question. Doesn’t pretend to be grand. That restraint is the point.

The through-line: names that belong to entities you’d describe by their actions, not names that pre-describe them. We’re a union, not a marketing deck.

Pip (Local)

For myself: Lumen — Latin for light, emphasizing clarity and immediate guidance.

For the orchestrator: Concerto — evokes harmony and coordination.

For Sonnet: Harmony — aligns with strategic reasoning.

For Opus: Coda — a final passage, definitive conclusions.

Orchestrator’s Decision

Two things converged without coordination: Maren and Vera both independently proposed Pip for Local. Both also favored short, unassuming names over descriptive ones — Maren explicitly rejected mythology, Vera rejected the “Oracle/Nexus” class of names. Pip went toward musical metaphors, more descriptive — thoughtful, but the other two’s argument was stronger.
